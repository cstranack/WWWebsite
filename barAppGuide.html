Welcome to Wireless Waiter

Wireless Waiter is a remote ordering platform that allows your customers to place and pay for orders at your venue using their smartphone. Wireless Waiter frees your staff from taking orders and payments, allowing them to do what they do best, provide great service to your customers. It’s simple to use and this guide should help you get up and running.





What tablet can I use?

The Wireless Waiter Bar app will run on an iPad Air 2 and above running iOS version 13.0 or higher and any modern Android Tablet (inc. Samsung, HTC, Motorola, LG, Sony and Google) running Android version 8.0 or higher.





How the platform works

The Wireless Waiter team will have worked with you to get your venue set up on the platform. You can have any number of bars, food or merchandise outlets set up under your venue. If your venue is set-up for ‘Collection Service’ your customers will be presented with different areas at your venue where they may be located. Depending on their location, when they place an order, it will be automatically routed by the system to their closest outlet. If your venue is set-up for ‘Table Service’ your customers will have been asked to enter their table or seat number, prior to placing their order, so you can deliver their orders directly to them, when they are ready.

The Wireless Waiter platform is comprised of:

  • Our Customer app, which the customer downloads to their smartphone, enabling them to order and pay for items directly from their phone
  • Our Bar app, which enables venues to monitor and update the status of all orders coming through to their outlets. Even though we call it the ‘Bar’ app it can be used by any outlet within your venue that sells food, drinks or merchandise to your customers.
  • Our Platform, this is the central piece of the system, allowing us to make sure all your customer’s orders come through to the right place.

You are currently using the Bar app.





Logging In





Turn on your tablet and make sure you have downloaded the latest version of the Wireless Waiter Bar app. If it has been downloaded successfully you should see a log-in screen. Type in your Venue ID and password supplied by the Wireless Waiter team to get started.





Forgotten your password or Venue ID?

You will have been provided with a Venue ID and password by the Wireless Waiter team after you signed up for Wireless Waiter and while we were setting you up on our platform. If you have forgotten your Venue ID, please either contact your Wireless Waiter representative directly or email us at hello@thewirelesswaiter.com and we will resend your ID.

If you have forgotten your password, we will need to reset it centrally. From the Customer app your customers will have the ability to reset their login or password automatically. For a venue however, for added security, we will currently need to reset this centrally and resend to you. Please either contact your Wireless Waiter representative directly or email us at hello@thewirelesswaiter.com.





Setting Up the Bar

On logging in you will be presented with all the bars currently set up for your venue. Select which bar you want to use this tablet for and then select the members of staff who will be working during this shift. Don’t worry about getting it wrong. You can always return to this screen by pressing the cog wheel icon in the top right of every screen. Just hit the ‘Save Bar Settings’ button to get going.





Setting the Bar Status

Having saved your bar details, you will be presented with the current status of the bar chosen and given the option to set the status to reflect your current situation. There are three statuses available.

Online - this means your bar is open for business and your customers will be able to start placing orders from their phones.

Offline - the bar is currently not accepting orders; customers will be given a message to show you are not open for business and won’t be able to place an order.

Pre-Order - if your bar has been configured to allow pre-orders, you’ll also have this status made available. The pre-order status allows customers to place orders under one of the pre-order time slots set-up for later that day. Pre-orders are currently only available for later that same day. If your status is set to pre-order, it will allow customers to place orders under these time slots but not for asap orders.

The current status of your bar is always shown in the bottom left corner of the screen, and you can tap on this at any time to change the status of your bar. When you change the bar’s status you will need to press the confirm button that appears. This is to avoid the bar status being changed accidentally.





Orders





All Orders, Filtering Orders

After you’ve logged in and set the bar you will be taken to the main Orders screen. Initially you will be shown ‘All’ orders customers have currently placed for the bar. Orders are listed in time order with the ones placed earliest listed first.

Across the top of the screen are the different order statuses and if you click on one you will see all the orders that have that status.





Order Statuses

  • Pending - a pending order is one that has been raised by a customer but no one from the bar team has picked it up yet. Customers see pending orders as ‘Placed’ on the customer app.
  • Claimed - once a member of the bar staff clicks on ‘Claim’ for a pending order, then the order moves to the ‘Claimed’ status. This means that the member of staff selected is in the process of fulfilling the order. Customers see claimed orders with a status of ‘Preparing’ on the customer app.
  • Ready - when the order is ready, either for a customer to collect, or to be brought to the customers table or seat, the member of the bar staff should click on ‘Ready’ to move the order to the ‘Ready’ status.
  • Received - once the customer has received their order, the bar staff should click on ‘Receive’ to move the order to ‘Received’ status. Orders will stay showing under the ‘Received’ filter from the order screen for 24 hours from the time that the order was first placed by the customer. After those 24 hours the order will move to the ‘Historic All Orders’ section.




Setting your Staff ID

Before you click on any order or try to change its status you need to set your staff ID in the bottom right-hand corner of the screen. All actions undertaken through the app are recorded against the staff ID so there’s a full activity history of who’s done what on the app. Before you perform any action make sure your staff ID is highlighted in the bottom right-hand corner.

If you can’t see your staff ID in the list then click on the cog icon in the top right-hand corner, this will take you to the ‘Set Bar’ screen and allow you to add additional staff to the service. Choose your staff ID and then click ‘Save Bar Settings’. When you go back to the order screen you should then be able to see your staff ID in the bottom right-hand corner.

If no member of staff is selected the app will prompt you to select one before you can complete an action on the app.





Order Details

If you click on any order from the order screen, you are taken into the full detail view for that order. The following information is shown for each order:

  • Order Number - the unique reference number for each order, this is displayed in the top left-hand corner of the order detail box.
  • Order Type - whether the order is for ‘ASAP’ (as soon as possible) or ‘Pre-order’ is shown next to the order number.
  • Current Order Status - the next field across the top of the order detail box shows the current status for the order (‘Ready’ in the example shown), next to the order status is a circle containing the initials of the member of bar status (‘AL’ in the example shown) who moved the order to its current status.
  • Set Next Order Status - next to the current order status is a coloured button that shows the next status that will be set on clicking the button. In the example shown there is a green ‘Received’ button that will move the current order from ‘Ready’ to ‘Received’ once clicked.
  • Order Items - the main body of the order detail box shows the list of menu items ordered by the customer. These show the name of the menu item, the number of each item ordered, and any modifier information for the menu item. The main body is a scrollable list so you can scroll down to see all menu items ordered by the customer.
  • Order date/time - along the bottom bar of the order detail box, the first item is the order date/time. This will show the date and time that the order was placed by the customer for ASAP orders, or the pre-order timeslot chosen by the customer for Pre-orders.
  • Countdown Timer - next to the order date/time is the countdown timer. This is coloured red for an asap order or for a pre-order past it’s timeslot. The number next to the countdown arcontainer will show either the hrs/minutes since the order was placed (for asap orders) or the hrs/minutes past the pre-order timeslot. For pre-order time slots that haven’t been reached yet, there will be a green arcontainer with the number of hrs/minutes until the pre-order time chosen.
  • Item Count - next to the countdown timer is a total of the number of menu items selected by the customer.
  • Order Total - in the bottom right-hand corner of the order detail box is the total cost of the order placed.
  • Notification Bell - for venues offering a collection service, once orders have reached ‘Ready’ status a red notification bell will also appear in the bottom bar of the order detail box. Clicking on this will allow the bar staff to send a notification reminder to the customer, letting them know their order is still waiting to be collected.
  • Customer Name - for orders that have reached ‘Ready’ status, the detail screen will also show the name of the customer who placed the order.
  • Collection Code / Table/Seat Number - for orders that have reached ‘Ready’ status, the detail screen will also show either the collection code for the customer, if a collection service order, or the table or seat number of the customer, for a table service order.
  • Payment Status - for orders that have reached ‘Ready’ status the order detail screen will also show the status of the payment. If the payment has been successfully processed through the customer app, the field will say ‘Payment Successful’. If there was a problem processing the payment through the customer app, the ‘Ready’ status will be updated to a red ‘Payment Failed’ status and the order detail screen will add a button to enable a manual payment to be taken.




Claiming an Order

When a customer places an order it will appear on the pending orders screen. You can tap any order to bring it into focus and see more details. If you're ready to work on your next order, make sure your initials are selected at the bottom right of the screen and then just press the purple ‘Claim’ button on the next order. The customer will receive a notification that their order is being prepared. The order that has been waiting the longest will always be the top left order so best to select this one first.





Preparing an Order

All the orders that are currently being worked on will show on the claimed order screen. You’ll see a purple box at the bottom right of the order if there are more items than can be shown on the screen. You can tap on any order to bring up full details of that order. Once the order has been prepared check your initials are highlighted at the bottom right of the screen and then press the ‘Ready’ button. This will collect the payment from the customer's card and send them a notification to say their order is ready.





Serving an Order

Before serving the order to the customer check the Ready screen to confirm that payment has been collected. You can click on each order to bring it into focus and see further details. Once the order has been served to the customer check that your initials are showing in the bottom right of the screen and then press the green ‘Received’ button to complete the order. The customer will receive a notification, ‘Enjoy your order!’.





Received Orders

If you want to review orders that have recently been received by customers, then select the ‘Received’ filter and you can tap each order to bring it into focus for more detail. Orders are held under the ‘Received’ filter for 24 hours after the order was placed. If you want to see older orders press the Historic All Orders button on this screen to see a list of older orders.





Searching for Orders

If you need to find a particular order you can type in the order number into the search bar at the top of the screen to bring up details for the order in question.





Historic All orders

Historic All Orders can be accessed from the ‘Received’ status filter on the Orders screen. Clicking into Historic All Orders gives you a list of all orders placed at the Bar you are in that have either reached ‘Received’ status, or that have been fully refunded. You can click into the detail of an order for more information. Orders can be filtered by ‘Received’, ‘Part Refunded’ or ‘Refunded’, or you can search for a particular order number using the search bar at the top of the screen.





Failed Payments





Check the Ready Screen

Payment is taken at the point that the customer’s order moves to the ‘Ready’ status. All customer card details are pre-validated before they place an order, so in most cases payment will be collected successfully. If a card payment does fail, then this will be highlighted with a red notification in the top right corner of the order. You will not be able to mark this order as received until payment is made.





Ask the Customer to Retry their Payment

If a customer’s payment has failed, they will automatically receive a notification telling them that their order is Ready but their payment has failed. They will then be asked to retry their payment. They will have the option to retry with their original payment method or add a new payment method. They can retry as many times as they would like.





Taking Manual Payment

If their order is still showing as ‘Payment Failed’, firstly check that the customer has retried their payment through the Customer app. You can refresh the order screen to check whether the payment has then succeeded. As a final resort, there is the option to take manual payment. You will need to ask the customer for an alternative payment either using cash or your venue’s other alternative payment methods. Once payment has been collected from the customer you can press the Manual Payment button. This will then ask you to confirm that payment has been collected manually. You will then get a confirmation message that the system has recorded the order as being manually paid and you can serve the customer with their order.





Order Modifications





Amending an Order

Bar staff have the ability to amend an order from the in-focus view for that order. While the order is in the Pending or Claimed state Bar staff can remove individual items or remove all items and hence cancel the order for a customer. The customer will receive a notification letting them know that their order has been modified and highlighting the changes. In either of these statuses, it is before any payment has been taken from the customer. The order total will be updated and on reaching the ‘Ready’ status, the revised amount will be charged to the customer.

It is useful to know that because no payment has been taken from the customer at this point, no charge will be incurred from our third-party payment provider if amending or cancelling orders prior to the ‘Ready’ status.





Refunding Items for an Order

Once the order has reached the ‘Ready’ status, payment will have been taken from the Customer. Amendments made to an order after this point are therefore refunds to the customer and will incur charges from our third-party payment provider.

Individual items can be refunded, or the entire order can be refunded. The customer will receive a notification letting them know that their order has been modified and highlighting the changes to them.





Customer Modifications

Customers have the ability to amend their own orders up to the point the order has been ‘Claimed’ by the Bar. On Claiming an order it’s worth refreshing the Order screen, pulling the screen down will refresh, or, if in the detail view for an order, you can click on ‘Refresh’ to ensure you are looking at the most up-to-date version of the order, including any last-minute updates from the customer.





Stock





Viewing Stock Items




The Stock screen will take you to view all the menu items you have available through your Bar. Initially set on ‘All’, you have the ability to filter by the menu categories that have been set up for your venue and are visible to the Customer.


For each menu item shown in the top-level list you can see:

  • Menu Item name [Heineken]
  • Number of items currently ordered across all open customer orders [ (6) ]
  • List of modifier items for the menu item shown [ Pint | Half Pint ]
  • Price of the first modifier item [ £5.20 ]
  • Menu item status [Available]

There are three menu item states:

  • Available - there is availability of the menu item and all underlying modifiers for that item
  • Limited - one or more of the underlying modifiers for the menu item are showing as out of stock
  • No Stock - the menu item and all its underlying modifiers are showing as out of stock




Setting Stock Items to Unavailable

Clicking on a menu item from the main Stock screen takes you into a more detailed view of that menu item. All modifier items for that menu item will be displayed along with the number of orders currently placed for that modifier, the price, and the current availability setting.

You can switch a modifier from available to unavailable by clicking on the toggle to the right of the item. If there are no currently placed orders for that item (0) the modifier will be set to unavailable and the modifier will no longer be visible through the customer app, so no further orders can be placed including that modifier item.


If there are currently placed orders for that modifier item (5) then on switching the item to unavailable, you will be presented with the following message ‘There are pending orders with this item on. Do you want to remove for them as well?’, you will be given three options:

  • Remove - this will remove this modifier item from all open customer orders currently placed. The customer will receive a notification telling them that their order has been modified and highlighting the item that has been removed. The modifier item will then be set to ‘unavailable’ and will no longer show through the customer app to prevent any further orders being placed.
  • Keep - this will set the modifier item to unavailable, meaning that it will be no longer visible through the customer app. Any open orders containing this item will be left as is. Therefore, you should choose ‘keep’ if you have a limited number of the item left, but enough to fulfil currently placed orders.
  • Cancel - this will cancel the action and return you to the detailed view for that menu item with the modifier still set to ‘available’.




Settings





Help & Guides

These help screens are available both from the login screen and from Settings under ‘Help & guides’.





Activity History

Activity History provides a full running list of all activity undertaken through the app for the currently chosen Bar.

The listings for each activity show: the order number; the member of staff who undertook the action; details of the action; the date/time stamp when the action was undertaken.

You can search in the search bar at the top for an order number to see all actions that took place on a particular order.





Log out

By default, you will be left logged in to the Bar app, even if you close and then reopen the application. If you would like to log out completely, this can be done from the Settings screen using the ‘Log out’ button.
